AI Transformation for UX

Turning AI from scattered experimentation into a practical UX capability.

RoleSenior Manager, Global Platform UX & Design
FocusUX practice, operating model, product strategy
ToolsChatGPT, Gemini, Figma, Miro
TimelineOngoing transformation

Overview

I have been leading the AI transformation for UX by moving the conversation beyond hype and into practical delivery. The focus has been to help the UX team understand where AI genuinely improves quality, speed, and strategic impact, while also protecting craft, judgement, and responsible decision-making.

This work has covered team enablement, workflow design, experimentation frameworks, and closer partnership with product and engineering. The goal has been clear: make AI a capability the team can use deliberately, not a novelty people try in isolation.

The Challenge

Interest in AI grew quickly, but without leadership it risked fragmenting into inconsistent experiments, duplicated effort, and unclear expectations around quality.

Scattered Experimentation

People were trying tools individually, but there was no shared model for what good AI-enabled UX work looked like.

Inconsistent Quality

Without guidance, outputs risked becoming generic, unvalidated, or disconnected from real user and business needs.

Unclear Workflow Fit

The team needed clarity on where AI supports discovery, synthesis, ideation, and communication, and where human judgement must stay central.

Change Leadership

Adoption depended on building confidence, reducing anxiety, and showing practical value rather than mandating tool use from the top down.

The Approach

Defined Where AI Adds Real UX Value

Established clear use cases for AI across the UX lifecycle, including research synthesis, framing opportunities, generating first-pass artifacts, critiquing flows, and accelerating communication with stakeholders.

Built Team Confidence Through Practical Adoption

Led the team through hands-on exploration of tools and workflows, focusing on repeatable practices rather than one-off demos. The emphasis was on confidence, critical thinking, and knowing when not to use AI.

Protected Craft and Judgement

Framed AI as a co-pilot, not a replacement. Maintained a strong bar for design quality, usability, accessibility, and strategic alignment, ensuring human judgement remained the deciding factor in every output.

Connected Practice Change to Product Thinking

Used the transformation effort not only to improve internal UX workflows, but also to shape how the organisation thinks about AI-enabled user experiences, capability gaps, and opportunities for future product value.

Outcomes

Shifted AI from Curiosity to Capability

The team gained a more mature, shared understanding of how AI can support UX work in meaningful ways, replacing isolated experimentation with stronger collective practice.

Improved Speed Without Lowering Standards

AI accelerated parts of the workflow, especially around synthesis, exploration, and communication, while design quality and strategic judgement stayed firmly human-led.

Created a Stronger Foundation for Future UX Leadership

By leading the transformation early and thoughtfully, I positioned UX to play a more active role in how AI is adopted internally and how AI-enabled experiences are shaped in future product strategy.
