Customisable Experiences

Enabling user customisation

RoleSenior UX Lead & Sign-off
Team3 UX Designers, 1 PM, 14 Developers
ToolsAxure, Figma, Miro, Airtable
Timeline9 Months

Overview

The Customisable Canvas project introduced a fully flexible dashboard-building experience within Sophos Central, allowing users to tailor their own dashboards with relevant widgets. Initially launched as an MVP, we iterated through multiple phases to refine the experience into a fully realised product. Replacing the legacy, rigid dashboards, this new approach enabled product and feature teams to publish their own dashboards and widgets while giving users the power to customise their views.

The Problem

Legacy dashboards in Sophos Central were static and inflexible, forcing all users into the same fixed view regardless of their role, priorities, or workflow â€” limiting the value they could derive from the platform.

Rigid Legacy Dashboards

Existing dashboards were fixed and one-size-fits-all, preventing users from prioritising the data most relevant to their role or workflow.

Slow Time-to-Insight

Users had to navigate multiple fixed views to access information relevant to them, creating friction and reducing decision-making speed.

The Approach

Research & Discovery

Before designing, we conducted comprehensive preliminary research:

Competitor Analysis

Analysed how other platforms approach dashboard customisation, benchmarking best practices and identifying opportunities to differentiate our offering.

UX Best Practices

Explored established UX principles for dashboard design â€” accessibility guidelines, visual hierarchy, and interaction patterns that support efficient customisation.

Exploring Technical Capabilities

Worked closely with engineering to assess the latest front-end technologies and frameworks. Landed on a hybrid approach â€” some features purchased via third-party integrations, some built in-house.

Collaboration with Development

Close collaboration with engineering teams was essential in assessing technical feasibility of design concepts. We aligned research with development capabilities to ensure features could be implemented efficiently.

Design & Iteration

The project followed a Research â†’ Ideation â†’ Design â†’ Test cycle. We launched an MVP first, gathered feedback from real users, and iterated across multiple phases. Key design decisions included drag-and-drop widget placement, resizable layouts, and role-aware visibility settings â€” enabling personalised views without requiring technical expertise.

Results

Flexible User-Customisable Interface

Delivered a highly flexible and intuitive interface that empowered users to create dashboards tailored to their specific workflows, priorities, and roles. This shift from fixed dashboards to a fully customisable experience significantly improved user satisfaction and engagement.

Surfacing Insights Efficiently

Users gained the ability to surface insights that mattered most to them â€” quickly and without unnecessary friction. Whether tracking threats, monitoring device status, or visualising licensing data, users could now create highly targeted dashboards that put critical insights front and centre. This reduced time-to-insight and empowered more informed, proactive decision-making.

Stronger Collaboration Between Design, Development & PM

The success of the project was underpinned by a deeply collaborative approach. Working closely with engineers to ensure technical constraints were accounted for in the design, and regular alignment with product managers to prioritise features and balance user value with delivery timelines.

Scalable Foundation for Future Innovation

The Customisable Canvas was designed as a strategic foundation for long-term growth. By building a modular, extensible framework, product and feature teams could contribute their own widgets and dashboards with minimal effort â€” accelerating the delivery of new value to users.
