Customisable Experiences

Enabling user customisation

RoleSenior UX Lead & Sign-off
Team3 UX Designers, 1 PM, 14 Developers
ToolsAxure, Figma, Miro, Airtable
Timeline9 Months

Overview

The Customisable Canvas project introduced a fully flexible dashboard-building experience within Sophos Central, allowing users to tailor their own dashboards with relevant widgets. Initially launched as an MVP, we iterated through multiple phases to refine the experience into a fully realised product. Replacing the legacy, rigid dashboards, this new approach enabled product and feature teams to publish their own dashboards and widgets while giving users the power to customise their views.

The Problem

Legacy dashboards in Sophos Central were static and inflexible, forcing all users into the same fixed view regardless of their role, priorities, or workflow - limiting the value they could derive from the platform.

Rigid Legacy Dashboards

Existing dashboards were fixed and one-size-fits-all, preventing users from prioritising the data most relevant to their role or workflow.

Slow Time-to-Insight

Users had to navigate multiple fixed views to access information relevant to them, creating friction and reducing decision-making speed.

The Approach

Research & Discovery

Before designing, we conducted comprehensive preliminary research:

Competitor Analysis

Analysed how other platforms approach dashboard customisation, benchmarking best practices and identifying opportunities to differentiate our offering.

UX Best Practices

Explored established UX principles for dashboard design - accessibility guidelines, visual hierarchy, and interaction patterns that support efficient customisation.

Exploring Technical Capabilities

Worked closely with engineering to assess the latest front-end technologies and frameworks. Landed on a hybrid approach - some features purchased via third-party integrations, some built in-house.

Collaboration with Development

Close collaboration with engineering teams was essential in assessing technical feasibility of design concepts. We aligned research with development capabilities to ensure features could be implemented efficiently.

Design & Iteration

The work moved through a deliberate four-part cycle that kept product ambition, technical reality, and user value tightly connected. Rather than jumping straight from requirements to polished UI, each phase created the input for the next - helping us reduce risk while building confidence in the direction.

01

Research

We started by understanding how users currently worked, what information they needed most often, and where the existing fixed-dashboard experience created friction. This included competitor review, best-practice analysis, and close collaboration with engineering to understand the technical boundaries of what could realistically be delivered.

02

Ideation

With that foundation in place, we explored multiple ways a customisable canvas could behave. We mapped possible interaction models for adding, moving, resizing, and configuring widgets, and compared different approaches to balancing flexibility with clarity. This stage helped us identify the core patterns that would make the experience feel powerful without becoming overwhelming.

03

Design

We translated the strongest ideas into a coherent product experience, defining drag-and-drop behaviour, resizable layouts, widget states, and role-aware visibility rules. The design work focused on making the canvas feel modular and intuitive, while ensuring it still aligned with the wider Sophos Central design language and could support future expansion over time.

04

Test

We released an MVP early, gathered feedback from real users, and iterated in phases. That feedback loop validated what was working, surfaced where users needed more guidance, and informed refinements to the overall interaction model. Testing ensured the experience was not only configurable in principle, but genuinely useful in day-to-day workflows.

The result was a customisable experience that felt robust without becoming complex - giving users control over their own workspace while preserving usability, consistency, and scalability.

Results

Flexible User-Customisable Interface

Delivered a highly flexible and intuitive interface that empowered users to create dashboards tailored to their specific workflows, priorities, and roles. This shift from fixed dashboards to a fully customisable experience significantly improved user satisfaction and engagement.

Surfacing Insights Efficiently

Users gained the ability to surface insights that mattered most to them - quickly and without unnecessary friction. Whether tracking threats, monitoring device status, or visualising licensing data, users could now create highly targeted dashboards that put critical insights front and centre. This reduced time-to-insight and empowered more informed, proactive decision-making.

Stronger Collaboration Between Design, Development & PM

The success of the project was underpinned by a deeply collaborative approach. Working closely with engineers to ensure technical constraints were accounted for in the design, and regular alignment with product managers to prioritise features and balance user value with delivery timelines.

Scalable Foundation for Future Innovation

The Customisable Canvas was designed as a strategic foundation for long-term growth. By building a modular, extensible framework, product and feature teams could contribute their own widgets and dashboards with minimal effort - accelerating the delivery of new value to users.
